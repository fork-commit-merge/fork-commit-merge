This short note shows how to typeset fractions, integrals, and matrices in LaTeX.

\section*{Fractions}
The fraction identity
\[
\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad + bc}{bd}
\]
shows how a common denominator combines two rational expressions.

\section*{Integrals}
A definite integral can be written as
\[
\int_a^b f(x)\,dx
\]
where $a$ is the lower limit of integration and $b$ is the upper limit.

\section*{Matrices}
A $2 \times 2$ matrix can be typeset as
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
  a & b \\
  c & d
\end{bmatrix}
\]
where each entry represents one element of the matrix arranged by row and column.
